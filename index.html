About Me

Professional summary

I’m a data scientist and data engineer with a strong interest in bioinformatics, archaeogenetics, machine learning, and building reliable data workflows that turn complex data into meaningful insights. I recently earned an M.S. in Applied Data Science with a biomedical engineering focus from the University of Florida, where I developed hands-on experience in Python, SQL, machine learning, statistical analysis, and end-to-end data pipelines.

My work combines analytical thinking with practical problem-solving across projects in genetic variant classification, cardiovascular disease prediction, regulatory DNA modeling, and applied machine learning. I enjoy working on projects that involve data cleaning, feature engineering, model development, visualization, and reproducible analysis.

Before transitioning into data, I served 8 years in the U.S. Army, where I developed a strong foundation in leadership, logistics, accountability, and mission-focused execution. That experience continues to shape how I approach technical work: with discipline, precision, adaptability, and a systems-thinking mindset.

I’m especially interested in opportunities at the intersection of data science, data engineering, healthcare, and bioinformatics, and I use this GitHub portfolio to showcase projects that reflect both my technical growth and my commitment to solving real-world problems with data.
